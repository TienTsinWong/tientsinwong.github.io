A Naive But Real-Time Shader for Drawing Cartoon with Controllable Edges

Tien-Tsin Wong, Ping-Hay Lai and Yiu-Kei Cheung
Submitted to Journal of Graphics Tools

Abstract

Cartoon generation from geometry model is useful in animation and computer game industries. In this tutorial, we discuss a real-time and naive cartoon shader implemented on a programmable graphics hardware. It first shades the object surface with a `quantized'' color tone. Then edges are drawn by applying simple convolution kernel. Since the edges are generated in image space, the thickness of the edges is controllable. We also demonstrate how to draw thick edges using the second pass of convolution. Resultant cartoon images are included for evaluation. This paper serves as a tutorial on how to develop efficient shader under the constrains of limited programmability and limited precision.
